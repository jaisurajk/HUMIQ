Dynamic Pilot System Design

ClearRoute coordinates real commuter commitments for the San Ramon to Sunnyvale pilot.

The website starts as an information and trust-building experience, then becomes a commuter coordination system. Every enrolled commuter must plan all five weekdays, commit to a strategy, and help the platform balance work from home, staggered leaving, carpooling, or other approved actions across the pilot corridor.

Pilot Objective

Measure how many commuters can shift behavior on one target corridor.

ClearRoute’s first release is centered on a single repeatable route so the team can recruit commuters, build trust, track strategy enrollment, and see whether weekly planning changes congestion patterns in a measurable way.

End-to-End Journey

Step-by-step commuter interaction and system response from the full flowchart set

1

Landing page to login portal

The public site introduces the pilot and routes commuters into a central login portal where they either begin sign up or log in as a returning user.

2

New-user sign up or returning-user login

New users follow the sign-up flow with phone verification and baseline setup while returning users simply enter credentials and go straight to the dashboard.

3

Structured sign-up onboarding

The onboarding flow captures phone, email, password, approximate home and work locations, weekly office and WFH days, and commute preferences including flexibility and carpool openness.

4

Plan every weekday

Monday through Friday must each have a selected action. The system blocks “complete” status and raises alerts whenever a day is left blank.

5

Choose departure or carpool path

From the weekly plan view, commuters either check departure guidance or view carpool options. The departure path includes departure time and reminders. The carpool path includes nearby carpools, passenger requests, driver acceptance, and pickup coordination.

6

Arrival, feedback, and admin oversight

Once commuters arrive, they provide feedback. The system records data, updates future departure logic when timing is too early or too late, refreshes plans as needed, and exposes reports to administrators.

System Requirements

Dynamic requirements the platform must fulfill

Onboarding

OTP registration and corridor qualification

The system must verify each commuter by phone number, capture route relevance, and confirm the user belongs to the San Ramon to Sunnyvale pilot population while preserving the sign-up steps shown in the signup flowchart.

Scheduling

Mandatory Monday to Friday planning

Every weekday requires a selected action. Any blank day triggers a visible alert and keeps the week in an incomplete status until fixed.

Behavior change

Balanced strategy participation

The dashboard must surface how many commuters are enrolled in WFH, staggered leave, carpool, PTO, and future approved options to support balanced adoption before the daily departure and carpool flows occur.

Trust

Clear participation visibility

Commuters should see enrollment numbers, their own weekly status, and system prompts so they understand the pilot is coordinated and active.

Departure Flow

Departure time and reminder support

The system must support checking today’s departure, showing the current recommended departure time, and letting commuters set reminders before commuting to work.

Carpool Flow

Passenger and driver coordination

The platform must show nearby carpools, allow passengers to request to join, allow drivers to accept requests, and support pickup coordination before the commute.

Admin operations

Reporting and intervention tools

Administrators need filters, route-level summaries, unscheduled-user reports, and adoption dashboards to manage the pilot week by week.

Arrival Feedback

Post-trip learning loop

After arrival, the system must capture feedback, record commute outcomes, and adjust future departure timing or generated plans when trips were too early, too late, or otherwise misaligned.

Feedback Input

Strategy suggestion intake

Users must be able to propose other strategies so the system can evolve without breaking the required weekly planning workflow.

Two-Application Model

Separated experiences for commuters and administrators

Application 1

Commuter experience

  • Public pilot information and signup call-to-action.
  • OTP verification and commuter profile setup.
  • Route and standard schedule collection from the signup flowchart.
  • Weekly dashboard for mandatory weekday commitments.
  • Departure reminders, carpool matching, and arrival feedback.
  • Enrollment visibility and new strategy suggestions.

Application 2

Administrator backend

  • Pilot signup funnel and verified-user counts.
  • Daily and weekly completion reporting.
  • Strategy balancing by day, company, or corridor segment.
  • Departure, carpool, and feedback funnel reporting.
  • Lists of users with missing weekday actions.
  • Review queue for commuter-suggested strategies.

Logical Architecture

Core services behind the dynamic system

1. Marketing Site

Public trust and pilot explanation

Communicates the purpose of the pilot, expected commuter participation, and benefits for the San Ramon to Sunnyvale corridor.

2. Identity + OTP Service

Phone verification and secure access

Handles user registration, OTP delivery and validation, login sessions, and basic account security.

3. Commuter Profile Service

Stores baseline commute details

Persists employer, home area, destination, normal commute days, and flexibility preferences used throughout the pilot.

4. Weekly Planning Engine

Validates required weekday actions

Checks that Monday to Friday all have assignments, computes completion state, and triggers dashboard alerts whenever days are left blank.

5. Strategy Allocation Service

Tracks adoption across options

Aggregates enrollments for WFH, staggered leave, carpooling, PTO, and future approved strategies so the pilot can stay balanced.

6. Commute Action Services

Departure, carpool, and arrival feedback logic

These services power today’s departure view, reminder creation, nearby carpool discovery, rider-driver coordination, feedback capture, and future plan updates.

7. Reporting + Admin Console

Operational insight for pilot management

Produces route reports, weekly participation metrics, unscheduled commuter lists, and suggestion-review queues for administrators.
